### Christiaan de Ridder

I am a marketer who builds. 15 years in B2B, ex-AWS EMEA,
now Head of Global Marketing at Nordcloud, an IBM company.

I work end to end. Product, marketing, brand, and the tools that
connect them. Most marketers stop at the campaign. I build the
thing the campaign points to, and the systems that run underneath.
The AI writes every line of code. I have not read one of them. My
job is to decide what ships and what dies.

That judgment is the part the machine does not have. It is what I
brought from marketing. The AI thinks like a developer. It
optimizes for code that is defensible, not for what the user gets.
I drag every decision back to the person using it.

CoNudge, a SaaS for personal trainers, is one place I test this.
Real users, real decisions, real failures. What I learn there, I
apply to the next build.

How I work: I trust no single source, including the AI. Three
models in parallel, mechanical checks as the judge, instinct as
the tiebreaker. A model that lies politely is the most expensive
tool you own.

What this means for B2B tech in Europe: the model is not the hard
part. The market is. EMEA runs on relationships, sovereignty, and
buying committees, not US-style funnels. A US vendor wins at home,
then stalls in Europe because the buyer works differently. Data
location, EU-hosting, NIS2 compliance: these are not footnotes
here. They are the sale. I build the revenue mechanisms most
marketing only talks about. I cut what does not move pipeline.

I write about building and deciding with AI, and about selling to
business in EMEA.
